#1

Lucia Mariani-Vena

An interview by Ivonne Karamoy

Lucia is co-founder, principal and digital strategist at Feast Interactive, managing director of Girls in Tech Toronto, a wife and a mother.

I met Lucia at the Yonge and Eglinton Starbucks at 8:30pm after she put her kids to bed. This is when she begins the second half of her day. Lucia had her second child nine months ago and is currently on maternity leave. After two kids she’s realized that she has to manage her time a little differently than others, making sure to work
when she is most efficient. In our hour-long conversation we discussed where and when her interest in technology began, her mentors, her family, the state of women in technology and how she stays tuned into her career while raising a family.

Parental leave in Canada

Mothers are allowed 15 weeks full-salary paid maternity leave. After that, they get half pay. Parents are allowed up to 35 weeks combined leave.

Tell us a bit about yourself and how you got your start.

I’ve been in the industry for about 10 years. I completed my Arts Management degree at the University of Toronto. That was where I started in web design. I landed a few gigs because they wanted someone with an artistic background who could learn to code on the fly. At the time, they were trying to get faculty members to publish their dissertations in electronic format so I started designing ebooks and educational websites. I eventually landed at St. George Campus working with the VP of Research doing web design and development to push out the exciting research that was happening at the University.
